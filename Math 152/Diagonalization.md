Diagonal matrices make things easier:
$$\begin{bmatrix}
\lambda_{1} & 0 \\
0 & \lambda_{2}
\end{bmatrix}^k = \begin{bmatrix}
\lambda_{1}^k & 0 \\
0 & \lambda_{2}^k
\end{bmatrix}$$
Using this , for any matrix $A$,
$$A = TDT^{-1}$$
where $$T = \begin{bmatrix}
	\vec{v_{1}} & \vec{v_{2}}
\end{bmatrix}$$
$$D = \begin{bmatrix}
\lambda_{1} & 0 \\
0 & \lambda_{2}
\end{bmatrix}$$
